# FMCSA Hours of Service (HOS) Regulations
## 49 CFR Part 395

### Property-Carrying Drivers (Non-passenger)

#### 11-Hour Driving Limit
A driver may drive a maximum of 11 hours after 10 consecutive hours off duty.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.3(a)(1)

#### 14-Hour On-Duty Limit
A driver may not drive beyond the 14th consecutive hour after coming on duty following 10 consecutive hours off duty. Off-duty time does not extend the 14-hour period.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.3(a)(2)

#### Short-Haul Exception (150 Air-Mile Radius)
Drivers operating within a 150 air-mile radius of their normal work reporting location and returning within 14 hours are exempt from the 30-minute rest break and ELD requirements, but must track duty status with time records.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.1(e)(1)

#### 30-Minute Rest Break
Drivers must take a 30-minute break when they have driven for a period of 8 cumulative hours without at least a 30-minute interruption. The break may be satisfied by off-duty or sleeper-berth time.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.3(a)(3)(ii)

#### 60/70-Hour On-Duty Limit
A driver may not drive after 60/70 hours on duty in 7/8 consecutive days. A driver may restart a 7/8 consecutive day period after taking 34 or more consecutive hours off duty.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.3(b)

#### 34-Hour Restart
The 34-hour restart must include two periods from 1:00 a.m. to 5:00 a.m. home terminal time (limited to once per week).
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.3(c)

#### Sleeper Berth Provision
Drivers using a sleeper berth may split the required 10-hour off-duty period: one period of at least 7 consecutive hours in the sleeper berth, and a separate period of at least 2 hours either off-duty or in the sleeper berth.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.1(g)(1)

### ELD (Electronic Logging Device) Requirements

All CMV drivers required to keep RODS must use an ELD unless exempt.

**Exemptions from ELD:**
- Drivers who operate under short-haul exceptions (150 air-mile radius)
- Drivers of vehicles manufactured before model year 2000
- Drivers who use paper RODS for 8 or fewer days out of every 30-day period
- Drive-away/tow-away operations where the vehicle is the commodity
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.22; 49 CFR 395.1

**ELD Technical Specifications:** 49 CFR Part 395 Appendix A

### Adverse Driving Conditions
Drivers encountering adverse driving conditions may drive up to 2 extra hours (13 total for property carriers).
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.1(b)(1)

**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.1(b)(2)

### Key Definitions
- **On-duty time:** All time from when a driver begins to work until relieved from all responsibility for performing work. Includes driving time, loading/unloading, inspections, waiting at shipper/receiver.
- **Off-duty time:** Time when driver is completely relieved of all duties.
- **Driving time:** All time spent at the driving controls of a CMV in operation.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 395.2
