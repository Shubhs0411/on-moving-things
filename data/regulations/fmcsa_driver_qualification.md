# FMCSA Driver Qualification (DQ) File Requirements
## 49 CFR Part 391

### Who Must Be Qualified
Any driver operating a commercial motor vehicle (CMV) in interstate commerce must be qualified under 49 CFR Part 391.
**Citation:** 49 CFR 391.1

### Driver Qualification File (DQF) — Required Contents
Per 49 CFR 391.51, each motor carrier must maintain a DQ file for every driver:

1. **Application for employment** (49 CFR 391.21)
   - 10-year employment history
   - Signature under penalty of perjury

2. **Motor vehicle record (MVR)** — at time of hire from each state where licensed in past 3 years
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.23(a)(1)

3. **Previous employer safety performance history inquiry** — within 30 days of hire
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.23(d)

4. **Road test certificate** or equivalent (valid CDL in correct class)
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.31, 391.33

5. **Medical examiner's certificate** (current, from FMCSA National Registry)
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.43, 391.45

6. **Annual review of driving record** (MVR from each state licensed)
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.25

7. **Annual list of violations** (driver self-certification)
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 391.27

8. **Clearinghouse query results** — pre-employment full query + annual limited query
   **Citation:** 49 CFR 382.701

### CDL Requirements
- Class A: Combination vehicles ≥ 26,001 lbs GCWR, towed vehicle > 10,000 lbs
- Class B: Single vehicle ≥ 26,001 lbs GVWR, or towing ≤ 10,000 lbs
- Class C: Hazmat, 16+ passengers, school bus

**Endorsements:**
- H: Hazardous materials
- N: Tank vehicles
- P: Passengers (16+)
- S: School bus
- T: Double/triple trailers
- X: Combination N + H

### Medical Certification Requirements
- Must have valid Medical Examiner's Certificate (MEC)
- Examiner must be on FMCSA National Registry
- Physical qualification standards: 49 CFR 391.41
- Medical certificate duration: up to 2 years (may be shorter based on condition)
- Self-certification categories: interstate/intrastate, excepted/non-excepted

**Disqualifying conditions include:**
- Vision less than 20/40 corrected in each eye
- Hearing: unable to hear forced whisper at 5 feet
- Controlled substances use
- Epilepsy (certain exceptions with specialist waiver)
- Insulin-treated diabetes (certain exemptions available)

### FMCSA Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse
**Citation:** 49 CFR Part 382 Subpart G

**Pre-employment:** Full query required before allowing driver to perform safety-sensitive functions.
**Annual:** Limited query required at least once every 12 months.

**Prohibited status:** Driver with unresolved violations in Clearinghouse may NOT perform safety-sensitive functions.
**Return-to-Duty:** Must complete SAP evaluation, treatment, and RTD testing.

### Disqualifying Offenses (49 CFR 391.15)
Permanent disqualification from driving CMV:
- 2 or more convictions for DUI in CMV
- Use of CMV to commit a felony involving manufacturing/distribution of controlled substances

Disqualification periods:
- 1 year: DUI, leaving scene, using CMV in felony (first offense); railroad crossing violations (1st-3rd)
- 3 years: Same violations involving hazmat
- Life (may be reduced to 10 years): Multiple serious traffic violations

### Record Retention
- DQ file: 3 years after driver leaves
- Accident register: 3 years
- Drug/alcohol testing records: 1-5 years depending on type
**Citation:** 49 CFR 391.51(d)
