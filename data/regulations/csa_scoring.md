# CSA (Compliance, Safety, Accountability) Scoring
## FMCSA Safety Measurement System (SMS)

### Overview
CSA measures the safety performance of motor carriers and drivers using data from roadside inspections, crash reports, and investigation results. The Safety Measurement System (SMS) calculates BASIC (Behavior Analysis and Safety Improvement Categories) scores.

### The 7 BASICs

#### 1. Unsafe Driving
Speeding, reckless driving, improper lane change, inattention.
**Intervention threshold:** 65% (carriers with passenger vehicles or hazmat: 50%)
**Data source:** Roadside violations + crash data
**Weighting:** Time-weighted, 24-month lookback

#### 2. Hours-of-Service Compliance
HOS log violations, ELD violations, duty status violations.
**Intervention threshold:** 65% (HM: 50%)
**Key violations:** Log falsification (severity 10), ELD malfunction (severity 7), driving beyond limits

#### 3. Driver Fitness
Invalid CDL, medical certificate violations, driver not medically certified.
**Intervention threshold:** 80%
**Key violations:** No/expired CDL (severity 10), expired medical cert (severity 5)

#### 4. Controlled Substances/Alcohol
Drug/alcohol use or possession.
**Intervention threshold:** 80%
**Key violations:** Driver under influence (severity 10), possession (severity 6)

#### 5. Vehicle Maintenance
Brake violations, lights, tires, cargo securement, coupling devices.
**Intervention threshold:** 80% (HM: 80%)
**Most common violations:** Brakes (severity 8), tires (severity 6), lights (severity 2)

#### 6. Hazardous Materials Compliance
HM packaging, placarding, handling.
**Intervention threshold:** 80%
**Applies to:** Carriers transporting HM requiring placards

#### 7. Crash Indicator
History of crash involvement. Uses preventable crash data.
**Intervention threshold:** 65% (HM: 50%)
**Not a direct regulatory violation** — relative measure vs. peer group

---

### How Scores Are Calculated

1. **Violation weighting:** Each violation has a severity weight (1–10) and time weight (last 6 months = 3x, 6–12 months = 2x, 12–24 months = 1x)
2. **Utilization adjustment:** Normalized per inspection to account for carrier size
3. **Percentile ranking:** Carrier is ranked against peers (similar operation type and size)
4. **Score = percentile** (0–100; higher = worse performance)

### Alert Status
When a carrier's BASIC score exceeds the intervention threshold, they receive an **Alert** status, which:
- Triggers FMCSA prioritization for investigation
- Is visible to shippers and brokers on FMCSA website
- May trigger insurance premium increases
- Signals need for corrective action

### Score Visibility
- **Carriers:** All 7 BASICs visible
- **Public (FMCSA website):** Only BASICs above threshold are displayed
- **DataQs:** Carriers can challenge data errors

### CSA and Carrier Vetting Best Practices
When vetting a carrier, check:
1. Operating authority (active/authorized)
2. Insurance on file (minimum $750K liability for general freight; $1M for HM)
3. Safety rating (Satisfactory, Conditional, Unsatisfactory, or Unrated)
4. CSA BASIC scores — any alerts?
5. Out-of-service rate vs. national average
   - National avg driver OOS rate: ~5.5%
   - National avg vehicle OOS rate: ~21%
6. Crash history (normalized per VMT)
7. Number of inspections (low count = limited data = higher uncertainty)

### Safety Rating System (49 CFR Part 385)
- **Satisfactory:** Adequate safety management controls
- **Conditional:** Some safety management controls are inadequate
- **Unsatisfactory:** Safety management controls are inadequate — carrier must cease operations within 45-60 days unless corrected
- **Unrated:** Has not been rated (common for new carriers or those not yet inspected)

**Important:** An "Unrated" carrier is NOT the same as a good rating. Due diligence requires examining CSA scores, crash history, and operating authority even for unrated carriers.
