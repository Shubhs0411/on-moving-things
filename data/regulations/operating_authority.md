# Operating Authority & Insurance Requirements
## 49 CFR Parts 365, 366, 387

### Types of Operating Authority
Motor carriers must register with FMCSA and obtain operating authority (MC number) to operate in interstate commerce.

**Authority Types:**
- **Common Carrier (Property):** Transports regulated commodities for the general public for hire
- **Contract Carrier (Property):** Transports regulated commodities under continuing contracts with specific shippers
- **Broker:** Arranges transportation for compensation but does not transport
- **Freight Forwarder:** Assembles/consolidates shipments
- **Household Goods:** Moving companies (additional requirements)

### How to Verify Operating Authority
Use FMCSA's SAFER (Safety and Fitness Electronic Records) system:
1. Search by DOT number or MC number
2. Check "Operating Authority Status" — must be ACTIVE
3. Verify insurance filings are current
4. Check for any safety fitness determinations

**Active status required:** If authority shows INACTIVE, REVOKED, or SUSPENDED, the carrier cannot legally haul for hire in interstate commerce.

### Insurance Minimums (49 CFR Part 387)

| Commodity | Minimum Liability |
|-----------|------------------|
| General freight (non-HM) | $750,000 |
| Hazardous materials (certain) | $1,000,000 or $5,000,000 |
| Household goods | $750,000 |
| Passengers (1-15) | $1,500,000 |
| Passengers (16+) | $5,000,000 |

**Cargo insurance (BMC-32):** $5,000 per vehicle, $10,000 per occurrence (household goods carriers)

### New Entrant Safety Assurance Program
New carriers must:
1. Complete New Entrant Safety Assurance Process
2. Pass safety audit within 12 months of registration
3. Complete drug and alcohol testing program registration
**Citation:** 49 CFR Part 385 Subpart D

### Drug & Alcohol Testing Program Registration
All carriers must register with a USDOT-approved consortium or implement their own program.
**Citation:** 49 CFR Part 382

### UCR (Unified Carrier Registration)
Annual registration required for carriers operating in interstate commerce.
**Citation:** 49 CFR Part 367

### IFTA (International Fuel Tax Agreement)
Required for carriers with qualified motor vehicles operating in multiple states.

### Cabotage Rules
Foreign (non-US/Canadian/Mexican) carriers may not transport freight between two US points.

### Broker Requirements (49 CFR Part 371)
- Must maintain $75,000 surety bond (BMC-84) or trust fund (BMC-85)
- Must keep records of each transaction for 3 years
- Must disclose compensation upon shipper/carrier request
